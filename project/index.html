The Birdstopher Collection of Fine Art

Our Paintings

"Birdstopher Birlumbus"

A piece to inspire the adventurous spirit to explore far-off places. Perfect to spruce up any space at all, but especially the captain's cabin of your 16th-century Spanish Galleon.

"Wingston Birdchill"

Wingston is a bird for troubled times, a willful beacon of hope for the overwhelmed. We recommend Wingston for the walls of executive offices, or lobbies in government buildings.

"Birdtoven"

A bird whose soaring melodies are matched only by his extraordinary temper, Birdtoven makes an excellent addition to any music room.

Oh, and if you happen to know of any prestigious concert halls that need a gigantic portrait in their atriums, please let them know we're happy to send one free of charge.

"Birdbraham Eggkin"

A remarkable figure in bird history, Birdbraham is best-known for his tireless fight for Eggmancipation. We recommend this portarait in poster form for the tents of any loved ones serving in 19th-century civil wars.

"Birdo Bagbeak"

An unlikely hero, portrayed here in all his heartbreaking innocence. This makes a first-rate addition to any underground dwelling in lush, peaceful places.

"Dumblebird"

An enchanting portrait of one of the best-respected members of the bird community, and a delightful oddment to decorate the walls of any well-aged European gothic architecture.

(DISCLAIMER: does not move or speak)

"Birdnigo Wingtoya"

He's hunting for a bird with one too many feathers in its right wing. He's got a pretty limited set of skills and a heart of gold. This portrait makes a great gift for swordsmiths and miracle workers.

"Tloucan Skyflapper"

A rare and exquisite portrait of a bird who lived a long, long time ago somewhere far, far away. He knows there is good in you.

"Flitzwilliam Birdcy"

One of the most absurdly wealthy birds in all of Derbyshire, he's a lot nicer than he looks. A stately portrait such as this is often very popular among gentleladies of the Georgian landed gentry.
